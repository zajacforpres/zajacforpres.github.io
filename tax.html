Sales Tax Inclusion

Shopping can be a tedious task for some people, while being a fun activity for others. However, one thing I think we can all agree on is that it's really annoying never knowing the exact amount you will pay for an item or collection of items, due to the fact that, in America, tax is not included on price tags.

Perhaps one could calculate it, but that requires work. And perhaps one could simply just wait until they are given the total at the cash register, but why should we, the American people, settle for something so ridiculous?

Sure, there might be some sort of psychological benefit to companies resulting from not showing sales tax on price tags. The item looks less expensive to a potential buyer, making them more likely to buy it, blah blah blah. But in the end, companies are made up of people, and those people need to shop too. And I'm willing to bet at least a few of them would be happy with knowing how much an item actually costs before they get to the cash register to buy it.

Therefore, if I become president I will make sure that taxes be included on price tags across the nation, so that the hardworking citizens of this great country won't have to be left in the dark, guessing about the price of the items that they want to spend their hard earned money on.

It's about time price tags displayed the actual price of an item, because that's what they're there for.
