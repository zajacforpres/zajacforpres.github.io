Pomegranate Awareness

There is a problem in this country. No, not the economy, or the environment, or corruption in politics. That is not to say these aren't problems, they simply aren't ones I'm focusing on right now.

The problem I am focusing on now, my fellow Americans, is pomegranates. You may be quite confused right now, because you might not understand why pomegranates are a problem. This is precisely the problem. Of course, pomegranates themselves are not the problem. Instead, it is the lack of awareness of pomegranates that is the problem.

Now, when you walk into a store, you see the usual. Apples, bananas, some grapefruit, plums, et cetera. However, do you see pomegranates? Some of you might, but I have had too many experiences where I have wandered aimlessly through aisles and aisles of fruit, searching for the delicious, tangible, edible paradise that is the pomegranate and have come up empty handed.

What is the cause of this madness? They cost a lot. Why? Well, for one thing, pomegranates only grow during certain times of the year. For another, large parts of the pomegranate are not edible, making the edible parts more expensive.

If I become president, I will take steps to change this. I will subsidize pomegranate farms, allowing us to become more self-sufficient as a nation and creating more jobs in the process. This will also help lower the price of pomegranates.

Now, maybe some of you wish there was something you could do right now. I wish there was. And in fact, there is. One problem about pomegranates that I did not mention before is that they not in high demand. Once people begin to demand pomegranates, their prices will go down. Therefore, my mission to you, the people, is to buy a pomegranate. Just one. Maybe two. Heck, even three if you end up liking them. Increase pomegranate demand and decrease pomegranate prices. You can make a difference, you do matter, and you can help in this nationwide effort to lower the price of pomegranates.
