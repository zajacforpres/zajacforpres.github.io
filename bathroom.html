Public Bathroom Reform

My fellow Americans, have you ever had to relieve yourself while you were in a public area? Have you ever acted on this need and used a public restroom? Have you ever, upon entering a public restroom, had multiple senses assaulted? The smell, the sight, and, the feel? (Bless the poor soul that had their sense of taste assaulted). Have you ever wondered why nothing is done to improve these repulsive restrooms? It all comes down to politics.

Nowadays in politics, there are issues that politicians focus on. Of course, many don't agree on these issues, but they are focused on. In the midst of all of these issues, politicians sometimes forget other important aspects of life. They expect us to deal with these loathsome lavatories while claiming to be for the people. Let me ask you, do you think a politician uses public bathrooms? Perhaps. Do they use them as often as a normal citizen? I'm sure they don't. This explains why they never address contemptible comfort stations that U.S. citizens must use all of the time.

However, I am not like other politicians. I don't have a college degree yet. I am going enough that I have only been able to vote in one election. I state irrelevant facts that only detract from my ethos appeal because I can. I have nothing to lose.

So why would I sweep important issues under the carpet or flush them down a disgusting public toilet? I am a nobody, indebted to nobody, speaking on behalf of the people. People who must cross their fingers hoping that the liquid on the floor is water. People who use multiple toilet seat covers and still feel unclean. People who walk into a stall and wonder how a human being could possibly make, and then proceed to forget about, the enormous turd floating in the toilet bowl.

It's time we stand up for our butts, America! The public bathrooms in this nation, they simply aren't working. Let me tell you, those things, they're freaking disgusting! Vote for me and I will do my best to bring you floors that aren't sticky, toilets that aren't icky, and public bathrooms that don't smell like the unwiped butt of a prune eating toddler!
